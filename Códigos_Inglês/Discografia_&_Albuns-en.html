Discography & Albums

Explore Ado’s main albums and singles. On the right side of the page you can listen to a Spotify playlist while reading interpretations and messages of some key songs.

Kyogen (狂言)

2022 • Universal Music Japan / Virgin Music

Ado’s debut album, featuring tracks such as “Usseewa”, “Gira Gira” and “Odo”. The songs mix rock, pop and a theatrical feel, dealing with social masks, frustration and identity.

Overall mood: intense and dramatic, almost like an inner stage play that shifts between outbursts of anger and moments of vulnerability.

Uta no Uta – One Piece Film Red

2022 • Collection of songs for the character Uta, sung by Ado.

Includes “New Genesis (Shinjidai)”, “Backlight”, “Tot Musica”, “Watashi wa Saikyou” and others. The tracks follow the character’s emotional journey in the movie, from idealistic dreams to darker, conflicted moments.

Overall mood: cinematic, blending pop, emotional ballads and epic songs tied to the One Piece storyline.

Meanings & Messages

Usseewa

For many fans, “Usseewa” feels like a big “I’ve had enough”. The lyrics pour out the anger of someone tired of hypocritical rules, of swallowing everything and pretending it’s fine. The song becomes a way to vent everyday frustrations.

Gira Gira

“Gira Gira” is about looking in the mirror and seeing something strange or “ugly” by common standards, yet still finding a kind of personal glow there. It’s about accepting that our light can be weird and still be valuable.

Odo

In “Odo”, the party and dance atmosphere hides a certain inner chaos. It feels like using music and movement so you’re not trapped only in heavy thoughts — dancing as a form of emotional survival.

New Genesis (Shinjidai)

Tied to Uta’s story in One Piece Film Red, “New Genesis” sounds like an invitation to build a new world your own way. The song balances dream and power, showing both the beauty and the burden of trying to impose your vision on reality.

Tot Musica

“Tot Musica” feels like a musical spell — something huge, dangerous and hypnotic at the same time. It conveys the sense of a force out of control, reflecting the story’s most intense moment where it appears.
